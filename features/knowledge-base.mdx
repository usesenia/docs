Senia's knowledge base is the brain behind her answers. It is built from the information you share during onboarding and can be updated at any time from the platform. The more complete and accurate your knowledge base, the more accurate and helpful Senia's responses will be — for every patient, in every conversation.

## What goes into the knowledge base

During onboarding you provide Senia with all the information she needs to represent your clinic accurately. This typically includes:

- **Services and pricing** — every treatment, procedure, or consultation you offer, with associated costs
- **Insurance and health plan coverage** — which insurers and health plans your clinic accepts, and any restrictions or authorisation requirements
- **Professional bios and specialties** — the name, credentials, and focus area of each professional in your clinic
- **Clinic hours and location** — opening times, address, and any practical access information (parking, nearby transport)
- **Most common patient FAQs** — the questions your receptionists answer repeatedly every day
- **Post-appointment instructions** — care guidance or preparation notes relevant to specific treatments
- **Clinic protocols and intake rules** — any rules that govern how bookings are made, deposits required, or policies patients should know about

## Training Senia

Training happens in two phases.

During **onboarding**, the Senia team works with you directly to build the initial knowledge base. You explain how your clinic operates — your specialties, protocols, tone, and the questions patients ask most often — and the team translates that into structured knowledge Senia can use.

Before going live, Senia is put through hundreds of automated test scenarios to verify she responds correctly across every situation. You review and approve the results, and only then does Senia start handling real patient conversations.

After launch, you can **add or update information** at any time from the **Settings** section of the platform — no technical expertise required. Add a new service, update a price, reflect a policy change, or upload new post-appointment instructions whenever your clinic evolves.

## How Senia uses the knowledge base

When a patient asks a question, Senia does not rely on generic AI responses. Instead, she searches your clinic's knowledge base to find the most relevant information before composing her reply. She then combines your clinic's specific details with natural, conversational language to deliver an answer that is both accurate and easy to understand.

This approach — sometimes called retrieval-augmented generation — means Senia's answers are always grounded in what you have told her about your clinic. She will not invent services you do not offer, quote prices you have not provided, or describe protocols you have not defined.

## Keeping the knowledge base up to date

A knowledge base is only as useful as it is current. Make it a habit to update Senia whenever:

- You add a new service or treatment
- Pricing changes
- A new professional joins the clinic (or an existing one leaves)
- Your clinic hours change
- Insurance or health plan agreements are updated
- A protocol or intake rule changes

The more accurate the knowledge base, the more confidently Senia can handle patient questions — and the less often she needs to escalate to your team for clarification.

<Tip>
  Review and refresh the knowledge base at least quarterly, and immediately whenever services, pricing, or protocols change. A quick audit every few months prevents outdated information from reaching patients.
</Tip>

## What to prepare before onboarding

Having the right material ready before your onboarding session makes the process faster and ensures Senia is trained thoroughly from day one. Prepare the following:

- Complete list of services with prices (or price ranges)
- All insurance and health plans accepted, including any coverage restrictions
- Full staff roster with each professional's name, credentials, and specialty
- Clinic opening hours, address, and access details
- Your top 20 most frequently asked patient questions, with the answers your team gives
- Any post-appointment or pre-appointment instructions for your most common treatments
- Deposit or payment rules that apply at the time of booking

<Accordion title="Why does preparation matter so much?">
  The quality of Senia's answers is directly proportional to the quality of the information she is trained on. A thorough onboarding session means fewer edge cases, fewer escalations to your team, and a more confident AI assistant from the very first patient conversation.
</Accordion>
